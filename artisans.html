Heritage Implements

Tools of the Trade

Our artisans rely on tools that have remained virtually unchanged for centuries, ensuring the authenticity of every knot.

The Shears

Used for trimming the pile down to a uniform height after the weaving is complete, revealing the crispness of the design.

The Comb (Dafeh)

A heavy metal or wooden comb used to beat down the weft threads, tightly packing the knots to ensure structural durability.

The Hook (Gholab)

A specialized knife with a hooked tip. It separates warp threads, pulls the yarn through, and cuts it in one fluid motion.

The Loom (Dar)

The foundational frame upon which the warp is stretched. Our vertical looms are crafted from seasoned hardwood to maintain perfect tension.

Our Roots

A Legacy of Threads

The weaving techniques utilized in our atelier have been refined over centuries, passed from master to apprentice in an unbroken chain.

Late 19th Century

The First Looms

The ancestors of our master weavers established small, family-run looms in the heart of the textile district. They began by weaving modest kilims utilizing locally sourced wool and early botanical dyes.

Mid 20th Century

Mastering the Silk Knot

As trade routes expanded, the guild gained access to fine Chinese mulberry silk. The weavers adapted their techniques, pushing the knot density to over 300 knots per square inch, creating highly detailed, luminous floor art.

Present Day

The Modern Atelier

Today, WeaveNest formally unites these generational families under one roof. We combine their ancient techniques with contemporary design software and ethical sourcing standards to create bespoke luxury for the modern home.

Next Generation

The Atelier
Apprenticeship.

To ensure the survival of traditional hand-knotting, WeaveNest fully funds a rigorous three-year apprenticeship program. We invite passionate individuals to learn directly from our master weavers, preserving the integrity of the craft while providing sustainable career paths.

The Process

How It's Made

01

Sourcing

Hand-selecting premium, long-staple wool and pure silk for maximum durability and softness.

02

Dyeing

Using botanical extracts and traditional vat methods to achieve rich, vibrant, and natural hues.

03

Knotting

Months of meticulous hand-tying on the loom, with up to 400 knots per square inch for complex motifs.

04

Finishing

Washing, stretching, and shearing the pile to bring out the rug's ultimate luster and precise texture.
